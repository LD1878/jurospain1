## Where should expats move in Spain in 2026?

The best region for your relocation depends on your residency path. For tech-focused remote workers, Málaga is the premier choice. For families seeking a balance of culture and cost, Valencia remains the leader. For corporate executives and those requiring high-speed rail connectivity to the rest of Europe, Madrid is the definitive logistical hub.

## Málaga: The Rise of Silicon Alley Europe

In our 15 years of handling visa audits, we have never seen a transformation as rapid as Málaga's. In 2026, the city is no longer just a holiday destination: it is a global tech magnet. 

The arrival of major R&D centers has created a unique ecosystem for Digital Nomad Visa holders. If your professional life depends on low-latency connectivity and high-level networking, the "Málaga TechPark" zone offers infrastructure that rivals London or Berlin. Logistically, the AGP airport provides direct links to North America and the Middle East, making it the most accessible coastal city for global citizens.

## Valencia: The Quality of Life Specialist

Valencia remains the 2026 favorite for Non-Lucrative Visa (NLV) holders and retirees. The city has invested heavily in "Green Infrastructure," making it one of the most walkable cities in Europe. 

While the tax environment is less aggressive than Andalusia (Valencia maintains standard Wealth Tax rates), the "Social ROI" is high. Public healthcare in Valencia is consistently ranked among the best in Spain. For expats who do not qualify for the Beckham Law, the lower daily cost of living in Valencia can often offset the lack of regional tax bonuses.

## Madrid: The Efficiency Hub for Business Residency

Madrid is the administrative heart of Spain. For those applying for the Entrepreneur Visa or those who need to be near the UGE (Unidad de Grandes Empresas), the capital is the only logical choice. 

The logistical advantage of Madrid is its "centrality." In 2026, the AVE high-speed rail network connects Madrid to almost every major Spanish city in under three hours. This makes it the ideal base for expats who plan to explore the country or conduct business across different provinces. Additionally, Madrid offers the most robust English-speaking legal and financial infrastructure in the country.

## Strategic Consideration: The North (Asturias and Basque Country)

A new 2026 trend is the "Climate Relocation." We are seeing an increase in high-intent queries for Northern Spain. Regions like Asturias and the Basque Country offer cooler summers and lush landscapes. 

While the "expat bubble" is smaller here, the infrastructure is elite. The Basque Country, in particular, offers a unique fiscal regime (Concierto Económico) that can be highly advantageous for certain corporate structures. If you find the heat of the south prohibitive, the north provides a sophisticated, high-service alternative with significantly lower tourist density.

### Can I live in one region and have my business in another?
Yes. You can reside in Valencia while your company is registered in Madrid. However, your personal income tax (IRPF) and Wealth Tax will be governed by the laws of the region where you spend more than 183 days per year.

### Is it difficult to find English-speaking schools in smaller regions?
In 2026, international schools are primarily concentrated in Madrid, Barcelona, Málaga, and Alicante. In more rural regions, you will likely need to rely on the local Spanish public or "concertado" school system, which is excellent but requires full language immersion.

### Which region has the fastest residency processing times?
The UGE in Madrid processes most "Large Business" and "Digital Nomad" residency permits electronically for the entire country. However, local police appointments for the physical TIE card are currently fastest in smaller provinces like Almería or Castellón compared to the wait times in central Madrid.
