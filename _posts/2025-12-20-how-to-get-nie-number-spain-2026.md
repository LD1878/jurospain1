## The #1 Confusion: NIE vs. TIE

Before we start, we must clear up the most common mistake expats make.

| Feature | NIE (The Number) | TIE (The Card) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **What is it?** | A tax ID number (e.g., Y-1234567-Z) on a white A4 paper. | A plastic biometric card with your photo. |
| **Does it expire?** | **Never.** It is yours for life. | **Yes.** It expires with your visa/residency. |
| **Does it prove residency?** | **No.** You can have a NIE and live in the USA. | **Yes.** It proves you legally live in Spain. |

*> **Key Takeaway:** You need the NIE first. The TIE comes later, after your visa is approved.*

## Method 1: The "DIY" Police Station Route (High Stress)

This is the traditional method: you fly to Spain, book an appointment, and go to the *Oficina de Extranjería*.

* **Pros:** Cheapest option (approx €10 govt fee).
* **Cons:** Finding an appointment is nearly impossible in major cities (Madrid, Barcelona, Malaga, Valencia). You may spend weeks refreshing the website at 8:00 AM.
* **The Process:**
    1.  Book *Cita Previa* online (select *Asignación de NIE*).
    2.  Fill out **Modelo EX-15**.
    3.  Pay the tax (**Modelo 790-012**) at a bank before the meeting.
    4.  Attend in person with your passport.

## Method 2: The Consulate Route (Slow)

You can apply at the Spanish Consulate in your home country (e.g., London, New York).

* **Pros:** You don't need to travel to Spain.
* **Cons:** Processing times are notoriously slow (4–8 weeks). Consulates are often understaffed and may require you to book an appointment months in advance.
* **Best For:** People with zero budget who are planning 6 months ahead.

## Which NIE do I need? (Resident vs. Non-Resident)

* **Non-Resident NIE:** For buying a house, inheriting property, or opening a bank account *without* moving to Spain. You receive a white A4 certificate. **This is what Method 3 gets you.**
* **Resident NIE:** If you are moving to Spain (e.g., Digital Nomad Visa), your NIE is usually assigned *automatically* during your visa approval process. You likely don't need to apply for a separate NIE beforehand unless you are buying property *before* your visa is approved.

## FAQs

**Can I buy a car with a Non-Resident NIE?**
Yes. You do not need to be a resident to own assets in Spain.

**How long is the NIE certificate valid for?**
The *number* is valid forever. However, the *paper certificate* (white A4) is technically valid for 3 months for administrative purposes. If you need a fresh copy later, it's easier to request a duplicate than to apply from scratch.

**Do I need an appointment for the Remote Option?**
No. We handle the appointments internally. You only need to visit a notary in your home city.
