# 100+ Spanish Legal & Expat Glossary 

Navigating Spanish bureaucracy requires precise language. This extensive glossary provides over 100 essential terms you will encounter during residency applications, property purchases, tax filings, and daily life, all explained in clear English.

## üöÄ 1. Immigration & Residency Terms

<div class="glossary-section">

    <details>
        <summary>NIE (N√∫mero de Identidad de Extranjero)</summary>
        <p>The **Foreigner's Identity Number**. A mandatory tax and administrative identification number required for nearly all legal activities in Spain (e.g., buying property, opening a bank account).     <details>
        <summary>TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero)</summary>
        <p>The **Foreigner's Identity Card**. This is the physical biometric card that confirms your official legal residency status (e.g., Non-Lucrative, DNV). It is issued after your visa is approved.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Non-Lucrative Visa (NLV)</summary>
        <p>A Spanish residency visa for non-EU citizens who demonstrate **passive income or sufficient savings** and commit to **not working locally**. <a href="{{ '/blog/spain-non-lucrative-visa-income-requirements-and-application-steps/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">NLV Requirements Explained.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Digital Nomad Visa (DNV)</summary>
        <p>A residency permit for **remote workers or freelancers** working primarily for companies/clients outside of Spain.     </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Golden Visa</summary>
        <p>A residency permit granted for a **significant capital investment** in Spain, typically a minimum **‚Ç¨500,000 real estate purchase**. <a href="{{ '/blog/spain-golden-visa-the-complete-2025-guide/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Golden Visa Guide.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Permiso de Residencia</summary>
        <p>The formal Spanish term for a **Residency Permit** or authorization to live legally in the country. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_nationality_law" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">See Spanish Immigration Law.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Residencia de Larga Duraci√≥n</summary>
        <p>The status of **Long-term residency**, granting permanent rights, usually acquired after five continuous years of legal temporary residency.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Certificado de Registro de Ciudadano de la Uni√≥n</summary>
        <p>The **EU Citizen Registration Certificate**. Required for EU, EEA, or Swiss citizens planning to reside in Spain for longer than three months.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Oficina de Extranjer√≠a</summary>
        <p>The **Foreigners' Office**. The government department that processes residency and immigration matters within Spain (as opposed to Consulates abroad).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Autorizaci√≥n de Residencia</summary>
        <p>Official **Residency Authorization** granted by the government.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Schengen Area</summary>
        <p>A zone of 26 European countries that have abolished passport and border controls at their mutual borders. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schengen_Area" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">Schengen Area on Wikipedia.</a></p>

    <details>

    </details>
    <details>

        <p>**Family Reunification**. The process by which a legal resident brings dependent family members to live with them in Spain.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Autorizaci√≥n de Regreso</summary>

    <details>

    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Arraigo Social</summary>
        <p>A special path to residency based on **long-term social integration** (3 years unauthorized stay, proven family ties, or a job offer).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Visado de Residencia</summary>
        <p>The **Residency Visa** sticker placed in your passport by a Spanish Consulate abroad, valid for entry to Spain to apply for the TIE.</p>
    </details>

## üè° 2. Property & Conveyancing Terms

<div class="glossary-section">

    <details>
        <summary>Escritura P√∫blica</summary>
        <p>The **Public Deed of Sale** (Title Deed). The official, final legal document, signed before a Notary, that formally transfers property ownership.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Nota Simple</summary>
        <p>A brief but vital extract from the Property Registry (**Registro de la Propiedad**). It confirms the owner, property details, and **any debts or charges** (liens, mortgages).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Registro de la Propiedad</summary>
        <p>The **Land Registry**. The official public institution that registers property ownership and all legal charges associated with real estate.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Notario (Notary)</summary>
        <p>A **Public Official** required to authenticate and certify legal documents, ensuring legality and full comprehension by all parties in a transaction.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Poder Notarial (Power of Attorney - POA)</summary>
        <p>A legal document signed before a Notary that grants a designated person (usually your lawyer) the **legal authority to act on your behalf** for specific tasks. <a href="{{ '/blog/power-of-attorney-in-spain-buy-sell-or-manage-admin-from-abroad/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">POA Guide.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Contrato de Arras</summary>
        <p>A legally binding **deposit or reservation contract** that secures a property sale, usually involving a deposit (typically 10%). <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arras_contract" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">Arras Contract on Wikipedia.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Contrato de Compraventa</summary>
        <p>The **Private Purchase Contract** (sales contract), used to formalize the agreed-upon terms between the buyer and seller before the public deed.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Due Diligence</summary>
        <p>The legal investigation carried out by the buyer's lawyer to check the property's legal status, debts, zoning, and conformity before purchase.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>C√©dula de Habitabilidad / Licencia de Primera Ocupaci√≥n</summary>
        <p>The **Habitation Certificate** or **First Occupation License**. A document issued by the local council certifying that a property is legally fit for human habitation.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Catastro</summary>
        <p>The **Cadastral Register**. An administrative register detailing the physical, economic, and legal characteristics of properties, used to calculate property taxes (IBI).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Valor Catastral</summary>
        <p>The **Cadastral Value**. The official administrative value of a property set by the *Catastro*, used as the tax base for IBI and other property taxes.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Comunidad de Propietarios</summary>
        <p>The **Community of Owners** or Residents Association, responsible for managing and maintaining common areas of apartment buildings or housing developments.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Hipoteca</summary>
        <p>The Spanish term for a **Mortgage** or secured loan on a property. <a href="https://www.rae.es/dh/hipoteca" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">Hipoteca definition (RAE).</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Plusval√≠a Tax</summary>
        <p>A local **Municipal Tax** levied on the theoretical increase in the value of the land when a property is transferred (sold). Paid by the seller.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Servidumbre</summary>
        <p>**Easement**. A legal right of access or passage granted over one person's property for the benefit of another's (e.g., a right of way).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Nuda Propiedad</summary>
        <p>**Naked Ownership**. The ownership of a property without the right to use or benefit from it (which belongs to the *Usufructuario*).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Usufructo</summary>
        <p>The legal right to **use and enjoy** a property and its income, without owning the property itself (the *Nuda Propiedad*).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Agente de la Propiedad Inmobiliaria (API)</summary>
        <p>A **Registered Real Estate Agent** (licensed professional).</p>
    </details>
</div>

## üí∞ 3. Tax & Financial Terms

<div class="glossary-section">

    <details>
        <summary>Agencia Tributaria (Hacienda)</summary>
        <p>The **Spanish Tax Agency**, often colloquially called *Hacienda*. The government body responsible for collecting state taxes.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>IRPF (Impuesto sobre la Renta de las Personas F√≠sicas)</summary>
        <p>**Personal Income Tax**. The standard tax residents in Spain pay on their **worldwide income**. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_income_tax_in_Spain" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">IRPF on Wikipedia.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>IRNR (Impuesto sobre la Renta de No Residentes)</summary>
        <p>**Non-Resident Income Tax**. Tax paid by non-residents only on income generated from **Spanish sources** (e.g., rental or imputed property income).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>R√©gimen de Impatriados (Beckham Law)</summary>
        <p>A **Special Expat Tax Regime** that allows qualified newcomers (like DNV holders) to be taxed as non-residents (flat 24% rate) on Spanish income for up to six years. <a href="{{ '/blog/spain-beckham-law-tax-guide-2025/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Beckham Law Guide.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Modelo 720</summary>
        <p>The mandatory form used to declare **overseas assets** (property, accounts) valued at over ‚Ç¨50,000 for Spanish tax residents.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Impuesto sobre el Patrimonio</summary>
        <p>**Wealth Tax**. A tax on a resident's net worldwide assets, applied above a certain regional threshold. Rates vary widely by Autonomous Community.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Impuesto de Sucesiones y Donaciones (ISD)</summary>
        <p>**Inheritance and Gift Tax**. A tax on assets received through inheritance or gifts. Highly regulated by the Autonomous Communities. <a href="{{ '/blog/spain-inheritance-tax-for-expats/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Inheritance Tax Guide.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>ITP (Impuesto de Transmisiones Patrimoniales)</summary>
        <p>**Property Transfer Tax**. A regional tax paid by the buyer when purchasing a **resale** (second-hand) property.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>IVA (Impuesto sobre el Valor A√±adido)</summary>
        <p>**Value Added Tax (VAT)**. Paid when purchasing a **new build** property (instead of ITP) or on most goods and services.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>AJD (Actos Jur√≠dicos Documentados)</summary>
        <p>**Stamp Duty** or **Tax on Documented Legal Acts**. A tax paid on notarized documents, often applied to new build purchases and mortgage deeds.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Declaraci√≥n de la Renta</summary>
        <p>The annual **Income Tax Return** that must be filed by all tax residents.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>NIF (N√∫mero de Identificaci√≥n Fiscal)</summary>
        <p>The **Tax Identification Number**. For Spanish citizens, this is their DNI; for foreigners, this is the NIE.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Cuenta Bancaria No Residente</summary>
        <p>A **Non-Resident Bank Account**, legally required for non-residents purchasing property in Spain.</p>
    </details>
</div>

## ‚úÖ 4. Bureaucracy & Administration Terms

<div class="glossary-section">

    <details>
        <summary>Empadronamiento (Padr√≥n)</summary>
        <p>The process of officially **registering yourself with the Census/Town Hall** (*Ayuntamiento*). Mandatory for long-term residents. <a href="{{ '/blog/empadronamiento-in-spain-complete-2026-registration-guide/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Empadronamiento Guide.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Ayuntamiento</summary>
        <p>The **Town Hall** or Municipal Government where local administrative tasks (like *Empadronamiento*) are handled.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Certificado Digital</summary>
        <p>A **Digital Certificate** that allows residents to securely identify themselves and digitally sign documents online with Spanish government agencies (AEAT, Seguridad Social). <a href="https://www.cert.fnmt.es/certificados" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">Official FNMT Certificate Site.</a></p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Cita Previa</summary>
        <p>The term for a **Mandatory Pre-booked Appointment**. Most Spanish government offices require you to secure a *Cita Previa* online before attending.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Traductor Jurado</summary>
        <p>A **Sworn Translator**. An official, government-certified translator whose work carries the same legal validity as the original document. Required for most foreign official documents.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Seguridad Social</summary>
        <p>**Social Security**. The government body managing public health care, pensions, and social benefits. Registration is required for all workers and residents applying for public services.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>N√∫mero de Afiliaci√≥n (NAF)</summary>
        <p>Your **Social Security Affiliation Number**, required to work or access the public health system.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Pensi√≥n No Contributiva</summary>
        <p>A **Non-Contributory Pension** granted to residents who have not paid sufficient contributions but meet income and residency requirements.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>DNI (Documento Nacional de Identidad)</summary>
        <p>The **National Identity Document** issued exclusively to Spanish citizens. (Foreigners receive the NIE/TIE).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Volante de Empadronamiento</summary>
        <p>A simpler, shorter version of the *Certificado de Empadronamiento* often used for internal administrative processes.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Apostilla de La Haya</summary>
        <p>The **Hague Apostille**. A certification that legalizes a public document for use in another country that is a party to the Hague Convention (required for many visa documents).</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Libro de Familia</summary>
        <p>The **Family Book**. A record provided by the Civil Registry detailing marriages, births, and deaths within a family.</p>
    </details>
    <details>
        <summary>Certificado de Nacimiento</summary>
        <p>**Birth Certificate** (often required in official Spanish translations for visa applications).</p>
    </details>
</div>
