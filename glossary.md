<h1>Spanish Legal & Immigration Glossary</h1>
<p style="text-align:center;">Your quick-reference guide to Spanish legal, visa, and immigration terminology — explained in plain English for expats.</p>

<!-- Example for Section A -->
<h2>A</h2>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Abogado</summary>
  <p>Lawyer or attorney. Required for most property purchases and complex immigration matters in Spain. <a class="cta" href="/services/">See our services</a></p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> AJD (Actos Jurídicos Documentados)</summary>
  <p>Stamp duty tax applied to certain legal documents, including new property purchases and mortgages. Typically 1-1.5% of property value. <a class="cta" href="/pricing/">See related pricing</a></p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Apostille</summary>
  <p>International certificate authenticating documents for use abroad. Required for most foreign documents submitted to Spanish authorities under the Hague Convention. <a class="cta" href="/services/">Our services can help</a></p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Autónomo</summary>
  <p>Self-employed or freelance worker registered with Spanish Social Security. Required if you work for yourself in Spain. <a class="cta" href="/services/#autonomo-registration">Learn more</a></p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Ayuntamiento</summary>
  <p>Town hall or city council. Where you register your address (empadronamiento) and handle local administrative matters. <a class="cta" href="/services/#empadronamiento">See our empadronamiento service</a></p>
</details>

<!-- Section B -->
<h2>B</h2>
<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Beckham Law (Ley Beckham)</summary>
  <p>Special tax regime allowing new residents to pay flat 24% tax on Spanish income instead of progressive rates for up to six years. Named after footballer David Beckham.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> BOE (Boletín Oficial del Estado)</summary>
  <p>Spain's official state gazette where laws, regulations, and official announcements are published.</p>
</details>

<!-- Section C -->
<h2>C</h2>
<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Catastro</summary>
  <p>Land registry office maintaining property records. <a class="cta" href="/services/#property-transactions">Learn about property services</a></p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Cédula de Habitabilidad</summary>
  <p>Certificate of habitability confirming property meets minimum standards for human occupation.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><span class="arrow">►</span> Certificado de Empadronamiento</summary>
  <p>Certificate proving your registered address at the town hall. Essential for visa renewals, healthcare, and school enrollment. <a class="cta" href="/services/#empadronamiento">See empadronamiento support</a></p>
</details>

</details>
