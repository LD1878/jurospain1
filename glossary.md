# Spanish Legal & Expat Glossary

Spanish legal terminology can be complex. We have compiled the most essential terms you will encounter during your residency application, property purchase, and relocation process, explained simply and professionally.

    <p>

## üè† Property & Financial Terms

<details>
    <summary>Escritura P√∫blica</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The **Public Deed of Sale**. This is the official, signed legal document, executed before a Notary, that formally transfers the legal ownership of the property from the seller to the buyer. It is the core document of any property transaction.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Nota Simple</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** A brief but critical extract from the **Property Registry (Registro de la Propiedad)**. This document confirms the current legal owner, provides the legal description of the property, and details any existing burdens (mortgages, liens, or claims).
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Contrato de Arras</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** A legally binding **deposit contract** or private purchase agreement signed by the buyer and seller before the final deed. It outlines the terms of the sale and typically involves a deposit (often 10%) that is forfeited if the buyer backs out without cause.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Notario (Notary)</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** A **Public Official** required by Spanish law to authenticate and certify legal documents, ensuring all parties fully understand and legally agree to the contents. The presence of a Notary is mandatory for signing the **Escritura P√∫blica** and granting a **Power of Attorney (POA)**.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Poder Notarial (Power of Attorney - POA)</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** A formal legal document, signed before a Notary, that grants a designated person (often a lawyer) the **legal authority to act on your behalf** for specific procedures, such as buying property or dealing with tax administration remotely.<br>
        <a href="{{ '/blog/power-of-attorney-in-spain-buy-sell-or-manage-admin-from-abroad/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Learn how a POA can simplify your Spanish admin.</a>
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>ITP (Impuesto de Transmisiones Patrimoniales)</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The **Property Transfer Tax**. This is a regional tax paid by the buyer when purchasing an existing (second-hand) property. The rate varies significantly depending on the Autonomous Community (region).
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Plusval√≠a Tax</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** A **Local Municipal Tax** levied on the increase in the official cadastral (land) value of a property over the time it was owned. In a sale, this tax is generally the legal responsibility of the seller.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>IBI (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles)</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The **Annual Property Tax**. This is a municipal tax based on the official cadastral value of the property, analogous to council tax or property tax in other countries.
    </p>
</details>

## ‚úÖ Bureaucracy & Administration Terms

<details>
    <summary>Empadronamiento (Padr√≥n)</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The process of officially **registering yourself with the Census/Town Hall** (Ayuntamiento) at your residential address. This is mandatory if you live in Spain for more than six months and is essential for most government procedures.<br>
        <a href="{{ '/blog/empadronamiento-in-spain-complete-2026-registration-guide/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">See our full guide to the Empadronamiento process.</a>
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Certificado de Empadronamiento</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The official **physical certificate** issued by the town hall that serves as proof of your registered address. It typically has an expiration (usually 3 months) for official use.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Cita Previa</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The term for a **Mandatory Pre-booked Appointment**. Most Spanish government offices (immigration, police, tax) require you to book a *Cita Previa* online before attending their office for any procedure.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Traductor Jurado</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** A **Sworn Translator**. This is an official, government-certified translator whose translation carries the same legal validity as the original document. Most foreign official documents must be translated by a *Traductor Jurado* for use in Spanish legal procedures.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Certificado Digital</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** A **Digital Certificate** that allows citizens and residents to securely identify themselves and digitally sign documents online with Spanish government agencies (Tax Agency, Social Security), significantly reducing the need for in-person visits.
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Aut√≥nomo</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The official Spanish term for **Self-Employed** or a sole trader. Any individual working for themselves in Spain must register as an *Aut√≥nomo* with the Spanish tax and social security authorities.<br>
        <a href="{{ '/blog/registering-as-autonomo-in-spain-social-security-and-taxes-explained/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Read about the registration process for the self-employed.</a>
    </p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Agencia Tributaria (AEAT)</summary>
    <p>
        **Definition:** The formal name for the **Spanish Tax Agency**, often colloquially referred to as **Hacienda**. This is the central government body responsible for collecting state taxes and managing the tax system.
    </p>
</details>
