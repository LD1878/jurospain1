## üöÄ 1. Immigration & Residency Terms

<div class="glossary-section">

    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">

        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Foreigner's Identity Number**. This is the unique, essential identification code required for virtually all major administrative and tax activities, such as **opening a Spanish bank account**, **buying a property**, or signing utility contracts. It is a mandatory requirement for all non-resident transactions. <a href="{{ '/guides/how-to-get-your-nie-number-in-spain/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Read our NIE Guide.</a>
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero)</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Foreigner's Identity Card**. This is the physical, **biometric card** that serves as proof of your legal residency status (e.g., Non-Lucrative, Digital Nomad, or Family Member of an EU Citizen). It is applied for *after* your initial visa approval and is subject to renewal.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">

        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A Spanish residency visa for non-EU citizens who wish to relocate based on **demonstrable passive income, pension, or substantial savings**. The central requirement is committing to **not engage in local professional activity**. It is typically renewed annually for the first two cycles. <a href="{{ '/guides/spain-non-lucrative-visa-income-requirements-and-application-steps/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">NLV Requirements Explained.</a>
        </p>
    </details>

        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Digital Nomad Visa (DNV)</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A specialized residency permit for **remote workers, qualified professionals, or entrepreneurs** whose work originates primarily outside of Spain. It provides a pathway to residency, along with access to the advantageous **Beckham Law** tax regime. It is initially granted for three years. <a href="{{ '/guides/spain-digital-nomad-visa-requirements-income-thresholds-and-taxes/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Digital Nomad Visa Guide.</a>
        </p>

        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A residency permit granted to investors who make a **significant capital investment** in the Spanish economy, most commonly through the acquisition of real estate valued at **‚Ç¨500,000 or more**. It offers expedited processing and flexible stay requirements. <a href="{{ '/guides/spain-golden-visa-the-complete-2025-guide/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">Golden Visa Guide.</a>

        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The formal Spanish term for a **Residency Permit** or authorization to live legally in the country, granted by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the *Oficina de Extranjer√≠a*. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_nationality_law" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">See Spanish Immigration Law.</a>

    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Residencia de Larga Duraci√≥n</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The status of **Long-term residency**, granting permanent rights to reside in Spain. This is typically applied for after five continuous years of holding legal temporary residency status, such as NLV or DNV.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Certificado de Registro de Ciudadano de la Uni√≥n</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **EU Citizen Registration Certificate** (often referred to as the 'green card'). This is mandatory for EU, EEA, or Swiss citizens planning to reside in Spain for longer than three months, proving their right of free movement.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">

        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Foreigners' Office**. This regional government department is responsible for processing many local immigration procedures, TIE applications, and renewals within the Spanish territory.
        </p>

        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> Official **Residency Authorization** granted by the government, which is the legal permission that allows a TIE to be issued.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Schengen Area</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A zone comprising 26 European countries that have abolished passport and border controls at their mutual borders. Holding a Spanish TIE grants the right to **travel freely** throughout the Schengen Area for periods up to 90 days. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schengen_Area" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">Schengen Area on Wikipedia.</a>
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Unidad de Grandes Empresas (UGE)</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Large Companies and Strategic Sectors Unit**. This specialized government body processes certain business-related and investment visas (like the DNV and Golden Visa), often providing a faster administrative route than standard applications.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Reagrupaci√≥n Familiar</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> **Family Reunification**. The legal process by which a non-EU citizen who already holds valid Spanish residency can bring dependent family members (spouse, children, dependent parents) to live with them in Spain.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Autorizaci√≥n de Regreso</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> **Return Authorization**. A temporary permit that allows a foreigner with an expiring TIE or a pending renewal application to leave Spain and **re-enter the country legally** without problems at the border control.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Hoja de Solicitud (EX-Form)</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Application Form**. The official, numbered government forms (e.g., EX-00, EX-15, EX-19) that must be correctly filled out and submitted for virtually every immigration procedure in Spain.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Antecedentes Penales</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> **Criminal Record** certificate. A mandatory document required for most initial Spanish residency applications, proving that the applicant has no prior criminal convictions in their country of residence.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Visado de Estudios</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A **Student Visa** for non-EU nationals enrolled in full-time, accredited educational programs in Spain lasting longer than 90 days. It is subject to renewal if the studies continue.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Arraigo Social</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A specialized path to temporary residency based on **long-term social integration**. It typically requires proving continuous stay in Spain for at least three years (even if unauthorized) plus documented family ties or a formal job offer.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Visado de Residencia</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Residency Visa** sticker placed in your passport by a Spanish Consulate abroad. This document is valid for entry to Spain, after which the holder must apply for the physical TIE within 90 days.
        </p>
    </details>

## üè° 2. Property & Conveyancing Terms

<div class="glossary-section">

    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Escritura P√∫blica</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Public Deed of Sale** (Title Deed). This is the official, final legal document, executed and signed before a Notary, that formally and publicly transfers property ownership from the seller to the buyer.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Nota Simple</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A brief but **vital extract** from the Property Registry (*Registro de la Propiedad*). It is the single most important document for due diligence, as it confirms the owner, legal status, and, crucially, details any **existing debts, liens, or charges** on the property.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Registro de la Propiedad</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Land Registry**. The official public institution that is responsible for maintaining the records of all Spanish property ownership and registering all legal charges associated with real estate transactions and mortgages.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Notario (Notary)</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A **Public Official** required by Spanish law to authenticate, certify, and give full legal validity to documents. Their mandatory presence ensures all parties understand and agree to the legal contents of the *Escritura P√∫blica* and Power of Attorney (POA).
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Poder Notarial (Power of Attorney - POA)</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A formal legal document, signed before a Notary, that grants a trusted, designated person (e.g., your lawyer) the **legal authority to act on your behalf** for specific tasks like signing the deed or arranging utilities, crucial for buying property remotely. <a href="{{ '/guides/power-of-attorney-in-spain-buy-sell-or-manage-admin-from-abroad/' | relative_url }}" class="glossary-link">POA Guide.</a>
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Contrato de Arras</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A legally binding **private reservation contract** that secures a property sale, usually involving the buyer paying a deposit (typically 10%). It sets a deadline for the final *Escritura* and outlines penalties for backing out. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earnest_money_deposit" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">Arras Contract (Earnest Money) on Wikipedia.</a>
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Contrato de Compraventa</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Private Purchase Contract** (sales contract). This is the detailed agreement used to formalize the terms of the sale, price, and conditions between the buyer and seller *before* the final public deed is signed at the Notary.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Due Diligence</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The exhaustive **legal investigation** carried out by the buyer's lawyer to check the property's legal history, title ownership, zoning permissions, outstanding debts, and tax compliance before a purchase is finalized.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">C√©dula de Habitabilidad / Licencia de Primera Ocupaci√≥n</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Habitation Certificate** or **First Occupation License**. A document issued by the local council certifying that a property is legally constructed and **fit for human habitation**, crucial for registering utilities.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Catastro</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Cadastral Register**. An administrative register detailing the physical, economic, and legal characteristics of properties. This register determines the *Valor Catastral*, which is the base for property taxes (IBI).
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Valor Catastral</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Cadastral Value**. The official administrative value of a property set by the *Catastro*. It is almost always lower than the market value and serves as the **tax base for IBI** and often other regional taxes.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Comunidad de Propietarios</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The **Community of Owners** or Residents Association. It is the legal entity responsible for managing, maintaining, and setting fees for the common areas (pools, elevators, facades) of apartment buildings or housing developments.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Hipoteca</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The Spanish term for a **Mortgage** or secured loan on a property. **Ley Hipotecaria** refers to the specific Spanish law governing mortgages. <a href="https://www.rae.es/dh/hipoteca" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="glossary-link">Hipoteca definition (RAE).</a>
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Plusval√≠a Tax</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A local **Municipal Tax** levied on the theoretical increase in the value of the land upon the sale or transfer of a property. By law, this tax is generally the **responsibility of the seller**.
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Servidumbre</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> **Easement**. A legal right of access, passage, or use granted over one person's property for the benefit of another's property (e.g., an established right of way or utility access).
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Nuda Propiedad</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> **Naked Ownership**. The legal ownership of a property *without* the right to use or benefit from it. This right belongs to the *Usufructuario* (usufruct holder).
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Usufructo</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> The legal right to **use and enjoy** a property and receive its income (e.g., rent) without owning the actual title (the *Nuda Propiedad*).
        </p>
    </details>
    <details style="margin-bottom: 1rem; border: 1px solid #ddd; border-left: 5px solid #b31b1b; padding-left: 15px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">
        <summary style="font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.25rem; cursor: pointer; color: #003366; padding: 10px 0;">Agente de la Propiedad Inmobiliaria (API)</summary>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <span style="font-weight: 600;">Definition:</span> A **Registered Real Estate Agent**. A professional, licensed title that requires passing an exam, although many working agents in Spain do not hold this specific accreditation.
        </p>
    </details>
</div>

## üí∞ 3. Tax & Financial Terms
