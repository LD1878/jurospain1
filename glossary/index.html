Immigration & Visas

Key terms for the Digital Nomad Visa, Non-Lucrative Visa, and EU Residency processes.

Insider Tool

UGE-CE

Unidad de Grandes Empresas

The specialized unit in Madrid that processes Digital Nomad Visas. Unlike local offices, they guarantee a 20-day resolution.

Insider Tool

Silencio Administrativo

Administrative Silence

A legal mechanism where a lack of response equals a decision. For the DNV, silence is Positive (Approval).

NIE

Número de Identidad de Extranjero

Your all-purpose ID number for tax and legal acts. You have the number for life, even if your residency expires.

TIE

Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero

The physical plastic card that proves your residency. It contains your photo, fingerprint, and NIE.

Toma de Huellas

Fingerprinting

The mandatory police appointment to provide biometric data for your TIE card after visa approval.

Resolución

Resolution Letter

The formal PDF document stating your visa is "Favorable" (Approved) or "Denegado" (Denied).

Recurso de Alzada

Administrative Appeal

The first level of appeal if your visa is rejected. It must be filed within one month of the denial.

Renovación

Renewal

The process of extending your residency. DNV renewals are currently for 3 years.

Empadronamiento (Padrón)

Municipal Registry

Registration at the Town Hall. Essential for proving address for TIE cards and public healthcare.

Certificado de Registro (CUE)

EU Green Card

The small green paper card for EU citizens residing in Spain. It does not have a photo.

Visado

Visa Sticker

The sticker in your passport allowing entry. Distinct from the "Autorización" (Residency Permit).

Espacio Schengen

Schengen Area

The 29 countries with open borders. Your Spanish residency allows 90/180 days travel here.

Taxes & Finance

Understanding Hacienda, the Beckham Law, and the Autonomo system.

Tax Hack

Ley Beckham

Special Expats Regime

A tax regime allowing new residents to pay a flat 24% tax on income up to €600k for 6 years.

Essential Tool

Certificado Digital

Digital Certificate

A digital ID file installed in your browser. It allows you to sign documents and access tax portals online.

Cl@ve PIN

Digital Key

A 2-factor authentication system for accessing Spanish government websites via a phone app.

Hacienda (AEAT)

The Tax Agency

The dreaded Spanish Tax Authority. Responsible for audits and revenue collection.

Residencia Fiscal

Tax Residency

Status acquired by spending >183 days in Spain or having your center of vital interests here.

IRPF

Impuesto sobre la Renta

Standard progressive income tax for residents (19% to 47%).

IRNR

Non-Resident Income Tax

Tax paid by non-residents on Spanish income (e.g., rental income from a holiday home).
